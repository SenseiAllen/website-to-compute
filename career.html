Career

Lee “Faker” Sang-hyeok turned pro in 2013 at age 17 and immediately won Worlds, launching a run that makes him the most decorated player in League of Legends. As T1’s long-time mid-laner, he holds six World Championships, an Esports World Cup win (2024), and ten LCK championships.

He has represented South Korea at the Asian Games (silver 2018, gold 2022) and earned major individual honors: Worlds Finals MVP (2016, 2024), Esports Player of the Year at The Game Awards (2017, 2023, 2024), Esports Player of the Decade (2025), and Riot’s first Hall of Legends inductee (2024).

Nicknamed the “Unkillable Demon King,” Faker blends strategic depth with enduring mechanical excellence. Beyond competing, he is a part-owner and executive at T1 (since 2020), shaping esports’ growth while remaining its defining competitor.
